About Present Perfect

Present Perfect we use when we tell no concrete time. We sometimes mix Present Perfect past simple with present continius that is when acting happened and happening now another thing, for example: I broke window and my mother shouted to me. Sometimes we add have/had, when we tell only about past simple, but about not concrete think.

When we say about something negative, we add not and go out, for example: I wasn't catching zombie, He wasn't driving auto.

When we asking we put was or have to the beginning of the sentence and it will go out, for example:Have you done your homework?.
